Full behavioural battery bounds the deviation-to-harm claim on OnSite

RQ012B deviation→harm = BOUNDED/NULL across the full behavioural interaction-failure battery — weak, consistent direction (more un-human → worse) but no robustly IPV-specific channel; too-passive worse than too-aggressive; passivity→deadlock is an unconfirmed hypothesis.

RQ012B tests whether an OnSite autonomous algorithm's deviation from the frozen RQ009 M3 human-IPV envelope predicts realised interaction-failure harm. The definitive result is bounded/null across the expanded behavioural battery: weak worse-direction associations appear, but no channel is robustly IPV-specific, BH-reliable, and adequately powered.

Plain-Language Result

In intuitive terms, more "un-human" driving weakly links to more interaction failures and worse official score deficits. The signs are unusually consistent, but the magnitudes are small and the formal controls do not support a robust IPV-specific effect. The clearest displayed patterns are near-miss/contact, deadlock/hesitation, and efficiency; hard-braking, jerk, comfort, and coordination are close to null. Too-passive deviation is usually worse than too-aggressive deviation, with contact as the main displayed exception.

Formal Verdict And Precise Wording

The accepted formal wording is: deviation from the human IPV envelope does not robustly or IPV-specifically predict realised interaction-failure harm on OnSite across the full behavioural battery. The direction is consistent in powered cells, but the battery does not produce a supported channel.

Uniform direction40/40 powered predictor×endpoint cells are worse-direction; the full 64-row family is 59/64 positive, with exceptions only in ultra-sparse E18/E19.
MagnitudeSmall. Full-family max partial r ≈ 0.205 at NEAR-MISS/CONTACT; most displayed simple-rank associations are ≲0.17.
Near-missNominal IRR≈1.22, p=0.0018, q=0.051, but it fails M2/context-only control; the signal is context-explained rather than robustly M3/IPV-specific.

Safety-Only First Pass As A Sub-Result

The earlier safety-only pass is retained as a narrower sub-result and is superseded as the headline by HA-4. It tested rare official_safety and collision/intervention endpoints. Official_safety had a directionally sensible raw Spearman r≈−0.12 (p≈0.06-0.08, n=245), but the pre-registered model did not support an IPV-specific association: frac_outside_90 increment 1.1595e-05, 95% CI [9.728e-08, 0.0013195], permutation p=0.7429, q=0.999; max_abs_exceedance_90 increment 0.0001303, 95% CI [6.687e-07, 0.0013279], p=0.6485, q=0.9947. Collision/intervention increments were effectively zero with p=0.3845/0.6941 and q=0.9422/0.999.

Honesty Notes And Limits

PowerRare hard harms limit the safety-only pass: 17/245 units have safety<100 and 16/245 have collision/intervention; team-effective n is about 19. The red team estimated raw MDE |r|≈0.18 at n=245 but ≈0.60 under clustering.
OOD transferInterHub→OnSite transfer is heavily OOD-limited: 72% of moments abstain, so the result applies to the in-support OnSite slice.
Context-dominant envelopeRQ009's counterpart-IPV channel is null, so M3 behaves close to a context-dominant envelope. Failure to beat M2 means a signal cannot be claimed as M3/IPV-specific.
Association onlyNo causal, counterpart-conditioning, team-ranking, or normative driving claim follows from this battery. Automatic-event counts are consequence evidence only when interpreted with controls and limits.
