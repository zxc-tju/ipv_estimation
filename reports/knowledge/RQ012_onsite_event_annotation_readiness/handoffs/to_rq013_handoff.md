# RQ012B → RQ013 handoff (beyond-safety incremental validity)

2026-06-28. RQ012B (OnSite deviation→harm) is COMPLETE and feeds RQ013 as a **bounded/null prior**.

## What RQ012B established (use as prior)
- On OnSite, an AV's deviation from the frozen human IPV envelope (RQ009 M3) does **NOT robustly, IPV-specifically predict realised interaction-failure harm** (events + official subscores). Direction is uniformly "more deviation → worse" but the link is **weak** (partial r ≲ 0.17), and nothing clears the negative-control battery + BH-FDR at a powered level.
- Near-miss is **context-explained** (fails the context-only M2 model) → not M3/IPV-specific (consistent with RQ009's counterpart-IPV null: M3 ≈ context envelope).
- The single coherent, all-control-surviving but **UNDERPOWERED** signal is **too-passive deviation → more no-progress/deadlock (E16)** — an unconfirmed hypothesis, not an accepted effect.

## Implications for RQ013
1. Do NOT assume an IPV-deviation→harm channel on OnSite; treat the consequence leg as **bounded/null** there. RQ013's beyond-safety incremental-validity case cannot lean on an OnSite M3-deviation→harm effect.
2. The OnSite consequence evidence that IS objective and available = automatic events + official collisions/deductions (descriptive), but they are **not IPV-specifically predicted** by deviation.
3. **Candidate hypothesis to carry (powered design needed)**: over-passivity → deadlock/no-progress, and possibly → near-miss/efficiency. A future test needs more units (RQ012B had only ~245 / ~19 team clusters; harms rare) and/or a within-dataset (not OOD) human reference.
4. Power + transfer are the binding constraints (rare harms; InterHub→OnSite OOD abstains 72% of moments). RQ013 should pre-register power and avoid the OOD-transfer + rare-outcome traps.
