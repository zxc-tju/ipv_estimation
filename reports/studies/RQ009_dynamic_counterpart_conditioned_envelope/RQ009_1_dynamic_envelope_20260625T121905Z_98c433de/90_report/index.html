The held-out test evidence supports a marginally valid online IPV interval envelope: M3 reaches 80/90/95% coverages of 0.816, 0.899, and 0.950 after OOD abstention. The practical adaptation signal is not in counterpart-IPV channels: context alone cuts 90% width by 42.3% versus M0, whereas M3 and the IPV-removed control differ by only -0.015% in 90% Winkler score with a case-cluster CI spanning zero. The interpretable conclusion is that adaptation is largely encoded in online kinematics and interaction context, not in a robust, cross-case counterpart-IPV performance increment.

Provenance And Code Parity

Phase 2 passed provenance and schema checks: the primary data hash matched, scene_unique_id + frame_index is unique, physical dt is 0.1 s, uncertainty semantics are source-proven, and the frozen windows are feasible. Phase 3 then assembled 6,397,266 perspective-anchor rows across train, guard_tune, calibration, and test; the hw=4 target lookup has code parity max absolute difference 0.0, and the independent matrix audit re-derived 400 sampled targets with max absolute difference 0.0.

Leakage safeguards are explicit: M3 excludes target-window outcomes, realized order, PET/offline risk, target-adjacent ego kinematics, and M4-only ego self-anchor fields. The matrix audit found no case split bleed and no future-peek signal; the largest numeric M3 feature-target absolute correlation was 0.115.

Calibration

The calibration stage fit M0-M5 plus controls with CQR and a train/guard OOD support gate. Non-crossing checks passed, M3 conformal validity passed, and the main test abstention rate is 4.778%. The 80% endpoint nudge caveat is retained because exact boundary ties affect the 80% count, while 90% and 95% are stable.

M0-M5 Evaluation

The large sharpness change happens when online context enters the model: at 90%, M2 reduces mean width by 42.3% and Winkler by 35.6% versus M0. Adding counterpart IPV in M3 changes Winkler by -0.015% versus M2, effectively no practical gain.

M3-vs-M4 Gate And Practical Null

The formal M3-vs-M4 gate did not escalate. M3 is 2.96% wider and 2.78% higher in 90% Winkler than M4, below the preregistered bars. More importantly, M3 and the IPV-removed control are practically indistinguishable at 90%: paired row-weighted Winkler difference -0.000211, case-cluster 95% CI [-0.001886, 0.001505], case sign p=0.863.

Exploration, Horizon, And Dependency Reconciliation

The 15 registered guard-tune exploration candidates did not clear both statistical and practical bars. The best point screen remained below the 0.02 ΔR² threshold (best dR² C04=-0.000912), and the best interval Winkler reduction was only -1.190%, far below 5%. The horizon sweep likewise found no guard-tune candidate requiring test confirmation. Dependency reconciliation corrected the earlier -0.11 to -0.125 curve: that curve used a different raw-current/Ridge estimand. The canonical HGB out-of-sample partial r stays near -0.04 and does not grow with horizon.

Ego Self-Anchor

M4 shows a small self-anchor benefit: at 90%, its width is 0.987 versus M2 1.009 and M3 1.016; its Winkler score is 1.384 versus M2 1.423 and M3 1.423. This is useful for sharpness, but not large enough to alter the counterpart-IPV conclusion.

Limitations

The result is marginal, not conditionally uniform. Across reportable subgroup rows, 126/264 fall outside ±3 pp. Leave-one-dataset-out 90% M3 coverage ranges from 0.749 to 0.991. Abstention is essential to the deployment contract and should be treated as part of the envelope, not as a cosmetic filter.

A separate target-distribution caveat is atom/boundary mass: the scored IPV target has a ~21.5% exact-zero atom (273,819/1,270,566) plus many exact-repeat and boundary values. This creates the 80% boundary-tie / 1e-10 endpoint-nudge coverage fragility and attenuates correlations, so it qualifies both interval-tie behavior and the interpretation of the practical null.

Robustness, Review, Red Team, And Replication

Perturbation sensitivity used outcome-blind train/calibration/guard-tune subsamples and found no validity break or practical null flip: M3 guard_tune coverage range 0.8754..0.9119; M3 paired relative Winkler gain vs ipv_removed range -0.541%..1.193%. Validity robust=True; counterpart null robust=True. Independent review passed with no blocking or major findings. Red team found no leakage or null-effect exploit, while retaining the 80% tie caveat. Clean-room replication reproduced M3 90% coverage and M3-vs-M4 direction, then reconciliation showed the apparent null divergence was a unit-of-analysis and route-labeling issue; all observed null-route effects remained far below the 5% meaningful bar.
