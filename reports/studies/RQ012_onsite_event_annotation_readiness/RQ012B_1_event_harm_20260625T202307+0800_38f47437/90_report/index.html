3. EH-2 - Sampling-Rate Sensitivity

Conclusion EH-2. The frozen extractor is sampling-rate sensitive; downstream event extraction must be pinned to the native rate.

PathRate comparisonNative primary countPerturbed primary countRelative deltaAttribution
Original wrapper decimate~10 Hz to 5 Hz20,11441,904+108.33%Runs through the frozen extractor's own extract_all_events(decimate_factor=2); real extractor rate-dependence.
Original wrapper interpolate~10 Hz to 20 Hz20,11415,573-22.58%Wrapper-only synthetic-midpoint perturbation; magnitude is method-limited.
Independent physical-time resamplingnative to 5 Hz20,07837,761+88.07%Principled uniform-grid resampling reproduces coarse-rate instability.
Independent physical-time resamplingnative to 20 Hz20,07813,971-30.42%Principled upsampling also reproduces finer-rate instability.

The mechanism is constructional: triggers use frame-difference jerk, rolling-median acceleration, and frame-spacing-estimated durations. E03 and E09 dominate the observed sensitivity. The carried condition is therefore binding: all post-M3 event extraction feeding stages 3-5 must use one frozen native rate, approximately 10 Hz median ego dt. Event definitions are not rate-invariant.
