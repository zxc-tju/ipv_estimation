# RQ008A Knowledge Synthesis

RQ008 asked what reproducible temporal structures appear in joint human IPV mean and uncertainty as InterHub interactions form, roles emerge, risk changes, negotiations resolve, and information disappears. The plan explicitly framed Wave A as open discovery only: split discovery from confirmation before exploration, search broadly inside discovery, apply mechanical and compositional controls, and freeze only a limited confirmation set. It also prohibited confirmatory wording, external outcome use, and manuscript claims from discovery-stage results.

## What Was Found

The knowledge-layer result is negative. The frozen evidence ledger has four rows, and only one supports an accepted substantive claim:

- `RQ008-EV-NEGATIVE-0OF24`: the Phase-7 control gate eliminated all 24 fixed discovery candidates.
- `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-NULL-PRIMARY`: a frozen primary null for possible Wave B, not yet decided.
- `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-FALS-MOTIF-008`: a falsification-only motif target, not a positive claim.
- `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-FALS-UNCERTAINTY`: a falsification-only uncertainty target, not an IPV-mean claim.

The accepted interpretation is therefore a boundary result: on InterHub sigma=0.1 discovery cases, no candidate directional temporal IPV structure survived the control gate. The candidate set covered 18 motifs, 2 alignments, and 4 role/uncertainty effects: 3 role/phase candidates plus 1 uncertainty/estimability candidate. Every row failed at least one relevant control. Motifs were mostly killed by reversed time; alignments were killed by source balancing and uncertainty-only clustering; role/phase candidates failed mechanical, pseudo-pair, duration, alignment, uncertainty, and estimability controls; the uncertainty candidate failed pseudo-pair, duration-matched, and estimability-matched controls.

That means RQ008A does not support a temporal IPV motif, a continuous feedback-adjustment story, reciprocity, lead-lag, risk-IPV coupling, hysteresis, or any IPV-mean temporal law. Descriptive atlas curves, alignment concentrations, role summaries, and motif labels remain useful as exploratory audit material, but not as accepted positive manuscript claims.

## Why The Negative Result Is Credible

The result is credible as a discovery-side negative because the run preserved the core governance gates. The discovery/confirmation split was established before temporal exploration, with 22,937 discovery cases and 15,291 held-out confirmation cases. The confirmation subset was physically protected and not opened. The plan hash was pinned as `3a94986587c3c30afa18b7a7c2151e272ac1382b24c43f8cbbf9a06b015f77db`.

The control phase was conservative and explicit. Candidates had to pass relevant members of the time-shuffle, reversed-time, pseudo-pair, duration-matched-null, random-alignment, source-balancing, uncertainty-only-clustering, and estimability-matched-control families. Passing only descriptive stability screens was not enough. This is why the headline is 0/24 survivors rather than "several promising motifs."

Independent checks support the negative boundary. The Phase-9 reviewer ratified the freeze with no blocking finding and set the protocol status to `FROZEN_RQ008A`. The red team returned `PASS_WITH_FIXES` with zero blocking findings. It found important nonblocking weaknesses, especially source composition, clustering instability, and Attack 10 false-negative risk, but these weaknesses narrow the claim rather than creating a positive result. The independent replication was partial: it agreed on atlas curves, role formation rate, a negative-control check, and the discovery/confirmation split, while finding change-point-bin divergence. Because change-point candidates were already rejected by controls, this divergence does not alter the accepted negative boundary.

## What It Means For The Program

RQ008A should lower confidence in broad, discovery-derived temporal-IPV narratives for this InterHub sigma=0.1 setting. The program should not claim that human IPV follows a discovered sequence of negotiation, reciprocity, escalation, yielding, or risk-coupled feedback based on this run. The safer interpretation is that apparent temporal structure in the discovery atlas is largely explained by control-sensitive artifacts: time-reversal invariance, source composition, duration, and estimation uncertainty.

The result is still useful. It provides a governance-grade negative boundary for future work: temporal IPV hypotheses need controls that distinguish directional temporal process from reverse-invariant shape and estimation artifacts. It also prevents the manuscript or downstream RQ009 work from inheriting unconfirmed motif labels as if they were social or behavioral laws.

## Open Wave-B Question

Wave B remains open and conditional. The frozen primary null, `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-NULL-PRIMARY`, asks whether the held-out confirmation set also produces zero fixed catalogue survivors after all controls and Holm correction. The two falsification-only targets, `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-FALS-MOTIF-008` and `RQ008-EV-FROZEN-FALS-UNCERTAINTY`, may only overturn that null if they pass the frozen high-bar controls on held-out data.

Before any confirmation opening, the pre-Wave-B Attack-10 amendment is mandatory. Either the protocol must add or pre-ratify a direction-sensitive companion motif statistic, or the null must remain explicitly scoped to directional temporal IPV structure that must exceed reverse-invariant shape. Without that amendment, the current reversed-time/SNR pairing is too direction-insensitive to rule out all reverse-invariant motif structure.

The knowledge-layer decision is therefore: accept the negative discovery boundary, reject all positive temporal-law claims from RQ008A, and keep Wave B frozen pending explicit authorization.
